What are Generative Adversarial Networks?
Definition
Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) are a class of generative models
consisting of two neural networks—a generator and a discriminator—that
compete against each other in a zero-sum game framework.
Core Concept:
Generator (G):Creates fake data from random noise
Discriminator (D):Distinguishes real from fake data
Adversarial Training:G tries to fool D, D tries to detect fakes
Nash Equilibrium:Both networks improve until equilibrium
Invented by:Ian Goodfellow et al., 2014
”The coolest idea in machine learning in the last 20 years”- Yann LeCun

===== PAGE 5 =====
GAN Training Process
Adversarial Game:
1 Train Discriminator:
Show real data, label as ”real” (1)
Generate fake data from G, label as ”fake” (0)
Update D to maximize classification accuracy
2 Train Generator:
Generate fake data
Pass through D (but don’t update D)
Update G to maximize D’s error (fool D)
3 Iterate:
Alternate between training D and G
D gets better at detecting fakes
G gets better at creating realistic data
Continue until convergence
Goal:Generator produces data indistinguishable from real data

GAN Objective Function
Minimax Game:
min
G
max
D
V(D,G) =E x∼p data[logD(x)] +E z∼p z [log(1−D(G(z)))]
Components:
x: Real data samples
z: Random noise vector (latent code)
G(z): Generated (fake) data
D(x): Discriminator’s probability thatxis real
pdata: Real data distribution
pz: Noise distribution (usually Gaussian)
Interpretation:
Discriminator:MaximizeV(correctly classify real/fake)
Generator:MinimizeV(fool discriminator)

===== PAGE 8 =====
GAN Training Challenges
Common Training Issues:
1 Mode Collapse:
Generator produces limited variety
Fails to capture full data distribution
Outputs same/similar samples repeatedly
2 Training Instability:
Oscillating losses
Failure to converge
Sensitive to hyperparameters
3 Vanishing Gradients:
When D becomes too strong
G receives no useful gradient signal
Learning stops
4 Evaluation Difficulty:
No clear convergence metric
Hard to assess quality objectively
Loss values not reliable indicators

===== PAGE 10 =====
DCGAN - Deep Convolutional GAN
Architecture Guidelines (Radford et al., 2015):
1 Replace pooling layers:
Use strided convolutions (discriminator)
Use fractional-strided convolutions (generator)
2 Use batch normalization:
In both G and D
Stabilizes training
Helps with gradient flow
3 Remove fully connected layers:
Use global average pooling
Fully convolutional architecture
4 Activation functions:
ReLU in generator (except output: tanh)
LeakyReLU in discriminator
Impact:Set the standard for stable GAN training

===== PAGE 13 =====
Progressive GAN
High-Resolution Image Generation
Training Strategy:
1 Start with low resolution (4×4)
2 Train until stable
3 Gradually add layers to increase resolution
4 4×4→8×8→16×16→...→1024×1024
5 Smooth transitions between resolutions
Advantages:
More stable training at high resolutions
Faster convergence
Better quality images
Achieved 1024×1024 photorealistic faces
Impact (NVIDIA, 2017):First GAN to reliably generate high-resolution,
photorealistic images

Style-Based Generator Architecture
Key Innovations:
1 Mapping Network:
Maps latent codezto intermediate latent spacew
Disentangles factors of variation
2 Adaptive Instance Normalization (AdaIN):

Controls different levels of detail

CycleGAN
Unpaired Image-to-Image Translation
Problem:Learn mapping between two domains without paired examples
Architecture:
Two generators:G:X→YandF:Y→X
Two discriminators:D X andD Y
Cycle Consistency Loss:F(G(x))≈xandG(F(y))≈y
Objective:
L=L GAN(G,D Y ) +L GAN(F,D X ) +λL cyc(G,F)
Applications:
Photo Painting (photos to Monet style)
Summer Winter scenes
Horse Zebra
Day Night

===== PAGE 16 =====
Pix2Pix
Paired Image-to-Image Translation
Problem:Learn mapping from input image to output image with paired
training data
Architecture:
Generator:U-Net architecture
Encoder-decoder with skip connections
Preserves low-level information
Discriminator:PatchGAN
Classifies image patches as real/fake
Captures local structure
Loss Function:
L=L GAN +λL L1
(Adversarial loss + pixel-wise reconstruction)
Applications:Edges→Photo, Sketch→Photo, Semantic labels→
Photo, Day→Night

GANs vs Diffusion Models
The Rise of Diffusion Models:
Why Diffusion Models are Gaining:
Training Stability:More stable than GANs
Mode Coverage:Less mode collapse
Quality:State-of-the-art image quality
Controllability:Easier to guide generation
GAN Advantages:
Speed:Single forward pass (fast sampling)
Real-time:Suitable for interactive applications
Established:Many variants and applications
Current Trend:
Diffusion models (DALL-E 2, Stable Diffusion, Midjourney) dominate
text-to-image
GANs still used for real-time and specialized applications
Hybrid approaches emerging
