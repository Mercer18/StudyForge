How Does GAN Work? - Step by Step
1 Initialization:
Initialize GeneratorGand DiscriminatorDwith random weights
2 Generate Fake Samples:
Sample random noise:z∼p z(z)
Generate fake data:x fake =G(z)
3 Train Discriminator:
Feed real samples: maximize logD(x real)
Feed fake samples: maximize log(1−D(x fake))
4 Train Generator:
Generate new fakes
Minimize log(1−D(G(z))) or maximize logD(G(z))
5 Iterate:
Repeat steps 2-4 until convergence

G

V(D,G) =E x∼p data(x)[logD(x)] +E z∼p z (z)[log(1−D(G(z)))]

===== PAGE 11 =====
Training Challenges
1. Mode Collapse
Generator produces limited variety
All outputs look similar
Fails to capture full data distribution
2. Vanishing Gradients
Perfect discriminator stops learning
Generator receives no gradient signal
Use modified loss: max G logD(G(z))
3. Training Instability
Oscillating losses
Requires careful balancing
Sensitive to hyperparameters
4. Convergence Issues
No clear stopping criterion
May never reach equilibrium
Difficult to evaluate quality
Solution
Use techniques like: Wasserstein loss, Spectral normalization, Progressive growing, Feature
matching

Introduction to Transformers
What are Transformers?
Transformersare a neural network architecture introduced in ”Attention is All You Need”
(Vaswani et al., 2017) that relies entirely on attention mechanisms, dispensing with recurrence
and convolutions.
Revolutionary Impact:
Replaced RNNs/LSTMs for sequences
Parallel processing (no sequential
dependency)
Better long-range dependencies
Foundation for modern LLMs
Key Applications:
Natural Language Processing (BERT,
GPT)
Machine Translation
Computer Vision (ViT)
Protein folding (AlphaFold)
Speech recognition
Core Innovation:Self-attention mechanism allows modeling relationships between all
positions in a sequence simultaneously.

===== PAGE 31 =====
Detailed Transformer Architecture
Input Processing:
1 Token Embedding:Convert tokens to vectors (d model dimension)
2 Positional Encoding:Add position information (since no recurrence)
Encoder Block (repeated N times):
1 Multi-Head Self-Attention
2 Add & Normalize (Residual connection)
3 Feed-Forward Network (2 linear layers with ReLU)
4 Add & Normalize
Decoder Block (repeated N times):
1 Masked Multi-Head Self-Attention (prevents looking ahead)
2 Add & Normalize
3 Multi-Head Cross-Attention (attends to encoder output)
4 Add & Normalize
5 Feed-Forward Network
6 Add & Normalize
Output:Linear layer + Softmax for prediction

Core Idea
Attentionallows the model to focus on different parts of the input when producing each part
of the output, weighting their importance dynamically.
Intuition:
Human reading: focus on relevant words
Not all input tokens equally important
Context-dependent weighting
Learn what to pay attention to
Advantages:
Capture long-range dependencies
Parallel computation
Interpretable (attention weights)
Types of Attention:
1 Self-Attention: Relate positions within
single sequence
2 Cross-Attention: Relate encoder and
decoder
3 Multi-Head: Multiple attention
mechanisms in parallel
Example:
”The animal didn’t cross the street
because it was too tired”

Self-Attention vs Cross-Attention
Self-Attention:
Q, K, V from same sequence
Relate positions within sequence
Used in both encoder and decoder
Example (Encoder):
Input: ”The cat sat on the mat”
Each word attends to all words
Captures sentence structure
Masked Self-Attention:
Decoder only
Prevent looking at future tokens
Ensures autoregressive property
Cross-Attention:
Q from decoder, K,V from encoder
Decoder attends to encoder output
Links source and target sequences
Example (Decoder):
Encoder: ”The cat”
Decoder generating: ”Le chat”
”chat” attends to ”cat”
