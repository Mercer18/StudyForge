===== PAGE 3 =====
What are Foundation Models?
Definition
Foundation models are large-scale AI models trained on broad data at
scale, designed to be adapted to a wide range of downstream tasks.
Key Characteristics:
Scale:Trained on massive datasets (billions to trillions of tokens)
Transfer Learning:Pre-trained once, fine-tuned for many tasks
Emergence:Exhibit capabilities not explicitly programmed
Homogenization:Single model serves multiple domains
Term coined by Stanford researchers in 2021

Foundation Models: Training Paradigm
Training Pipeline:
1 Pre-training Phase
Unsupervised/self-supervised learning
Massive corpus (web text, books, code, etc.)
Learn general patterns and representations
2 Fine-tuning Phase
Task-specific adaptation
Smaller labeled datasets
Optimize for specific objectives
3 Alignment Phase(Optional)
RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)
Constitutional AI
Safety and ethical alignment
